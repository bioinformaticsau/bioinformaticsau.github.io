About IQBA

The Institute of Quantum Bioinformatics Authority (IQBA) was established in 2015 as an independent scientific documentation and archival organization. It was founded by a group of interdisciplinary researchers and data custodians from fields including quantum physics, computational biology, and informatics. The institute’s purpose is to register and recognize first-of-its-kind discoveries that emerge at the interface of quantum mechanics and biological structure, particularly in domains where mainstream publication timelines may hinder early recognition.

What We Do

IQBA maintains a timestamped public registry of scientific disclosures in the field of bioinformatics — spanning molecular, systems, evolutionary, and computational branches. We issue recognition certificates for pioneering models, computational frameworks, or theoretical constructs that establish novel relationships between physical systems and biological information processing.

These certificates are awardds that function as proof-of-priority — showing that a given idea, model, or formalism was first publicly disclosed by the certificate holder, at a verifiable time and place.

How to Apply for Certification

To apply for an IQBA recognition certificate, you must provide documented evidence of formal public disclosure. This can include:

  • A valid DOI associated with your paper, model, or dataset
  • Proof of recognition or acknowledgment by academic or scientific institutions
  • Link to a peer-reviewed or publicly hosted preprint version
  • Identification or testimony of domain experts or peer reviewers

Once this information is prepared, applicants should request their university or institute to contact IQBA directly for formal processing and issuance of a timestamped certification. All applications undergo verification before registration.
